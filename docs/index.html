FloodWatch Ghana
GeoBuilders Africa · v1.0 · Greater Accra Region

What is this map?

FloodWatch Ghana is an open source flood risk intelligence system for Greater Accra. It combines five satellite datasets into a single risk score (0–1) for each of the region's 29 districts, updated monthly. A higher score means a district is more structurally vulnerable to flooding.

What does the risk score mean?

0.0 – 0.33Low Risk
0.33 – 0.67Moderate Risk
0.67 – 1.0High Risk

The score reflects chronic structural vulnerability — areas that are low-lying, poorly drained, near water bodies, and covered by impervious surfaces. It does not predict individual rainfall events.

Validation — May 2025

FloodWatch v1.0 underwent qualitative validation against the May 18, 2025 Greater Accra flood event (132mm rainfall, 4 deaths, 3,000+ displaced). Flooded districts averaged a mean risk of 0.598 vs 0.587 for non-flooded districts. All 3 chronically high-risk flood zones were correctly ranked in the top 4 districts. Quantitative Sentinel-1 SAR validation is planned for v1.1.

How to use

Click any district to see detailed risk statistics. Use the layer controls (top left) to toggle layers and adjust opacity. The map updates monthly as new rainfall data becomes available.
