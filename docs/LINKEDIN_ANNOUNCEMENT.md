# LinkedIn Announcement Draft: FloodWatch Ghana v0.1

**Headline: Why I built an open-source "Base Map" for Flood Risk in Greater Accra 🇬🇭**

Accra is no stranger to floods. But while we have great tools for real-time alerts—like Google Flood Hub—there has been a missing piece for the people building our city: **The Long-Term Baseline.**

Today, I’m excited to announce **FloodWatch Ghana v0.1**.

Unlike real-time apps that tell you when to run, FloodWatch tells you where to build. It’s a high-resolution (30m) intelligence pipeline that quantifies the **chronic structural vulnerability** of every district in Greater Accra.

### 📍 The "Base Map" vs. The "Live Layer"
- **Google Flood Hub (The Live Layer):** Tells you if a river is rising *right now*. Perfect for citizens and emergency response.
- **FloodWatch (The Base Map):** Tells you if a district is naturally prone to flooding due to its elevation, slope, and land cover. Perfect for urban planners, architects, and long-term infrastructure strategy.

### 🚀 What's in v0.1?
- **Weighted Risk Model:** 30% Elevation, 25% Rainfall, 20% Slope, 15% Imperviousness, 10% Water Proximity.
- **District Leaderboard:** See how all 29 districts of Greater Accra rank (shoutout to Ablekuma West and Weija Gbawe for being our top high-risk zones).
- **Interactive Search:** You can now search for any neighborhood (Kaneshie, Dansoman, Tema) to see the underlying vulnerability.
- **Open Source:** The entire pipeline—from ingestion to the TiTiler server—is open for the community to build upon.

This is just the beginning. While v0.1 is a static baseline for planning, v1.1 will bring the "Live Layer" and historical satellite validation.
